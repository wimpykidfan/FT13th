ABOUT US

AOT Leading The #FixThe13thNY Campaign

The #FixThe13thNY campaign is re-launching on America On Trial (AOT). The mission of the campaign #FixThe13thNY campaign is to end legal slavery in New York State by pushing a bill that supports ending legal slavery. This campaign is a grassroot & conscious campaign.

Our Resolution

We confide social progress. That's why we gather and raise our voice for every social causes.

The Thirteenth Amendment Loophole

In 1865, the 13th Amendment to the U.S. Constitution abolished slavery in most, but not all, cases. It remained legal “as a punishment for crime.”

The implications for individuals who are incarcerated in New York State

In New York State, incarcerated people are paid far below the minimum wage. On average, detainees in New York State prisons earn approximately $0.65 an hour, with some earning as little as $0.16 an hour.
Incarcerated people are forced to work without any federal and state employment protections,and far beyond minimum wage. They do not receive paid sick leave, mandatory overtime for unsalaried employees, OSHA protections, or unionization rights.

How do we start to create fair working conditions for incarcerated workers in New York State?

The #FixThe13thNY campaign is asking the State Assembly and State Senate to ensure the incarcerated are not forced into labor by passing legislation to prohibit it. This legislation must include an absolute ban on incarcerated individuals being compelled to work against their will.
In addition, a living-wage standard should be introduced into prisons, one which includes a meaningful minimum wage, labor protections, and protection from abuses. A good start would be for New York State to raise wages to Minimum Wage for incarcerated people.


Vidal Guzman
Executive Director & CEO
